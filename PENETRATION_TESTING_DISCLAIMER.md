## 4. Ethical Guidelines

### CORE PRINCIPLES:

1. **Respect Privacy**: Do not access, copy, or distribute sensitive data without explicit authorization
2. **Minimize Damage**: Use non-destructive testing methods whenever possible
3. **Report Responsibly**: Follow responsible disclosure practices for any vulnerabilities found
4. **Maintain Integrity**: Do not misuse access or privileges obtained during testing
5. **Document Everything**: Keep thorough records of authorization, scope, and activities

### PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES:

❌ Testing systems without authorization
❌ Exceeding authorized scope
❌ Causing unnecessary disruption or damage
❌ Exploiting vulnerabilities for personal gain
❌ Sharing confidential information obtained during testing
❌ Using techniques for malicious purposes
❌ Testing critical infrastructure (hospitals, emergency services, etc.) without explicit authorization

---

## 5. Responsible Disclosure

### IF YOU DISCOVER A VULNERABILITY:

1. **Stop testing** that specific vulnerability once confirmed
2. **Document the finding** with proof of concept (without causing damage)
3. **Report to the vendor/owner** through responsible disclosure channels
4. **Allow reasonable time** for remediation before public disclosure
5. **Follow industry standards** (e.g., ISO 29147, FIRST)
6. **Never exploit** a vulnerability for any purpose other than demonstrating it to the owner

### DO NOT:

- Publicly disclose vulnerabilities without allowing remediation time
- Sell vulnerabilities to third parties
- Exploit vulnerabilities for any purpose
- Share vulnerability details with unauthorized parties
